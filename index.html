William Shakepeare's Sonnets consisits of 154 poems, each fourteen lines long (three quatrains followed by a rhyming couplet). Sonnet 18 is perhaps the most famous, and begins like this:

Shall I compare thee to a summer'd day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of may
And summer's lease hath all to short a date.
